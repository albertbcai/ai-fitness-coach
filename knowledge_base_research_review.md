## The Ambiguity Problem

**You're absolutely right:** Exercise science has a lot of ambiguity because:

1. **Individual Variation is Huge**
   - Genetics, experience, recovery capacity all vary
   - What works for one person doesn't work for another
   - Guidelines are just starting points

2. **Conflicting Research**
   - Some studies show X, others show Y
   - Meta-analyses try to find patterns, but individual studies conflict
   - The field is still evolving

3. **Context Matters**
   - Training age (beginner vs. advanced)
   - Goals (strength vs. hypertrophy vs. endurance)
   - Lifestyle (nutrition, sleep, stress)
   - All affect what's "optimal"

4. **Research Limitations**
   - Most studies are short-term (8-12 weeks)
   - Most are on untrained individuals
   - Real-world application is different from lab conditions
